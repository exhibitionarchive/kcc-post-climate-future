About

Our fascination with the future and technology is imbued with positive wonders about how humans can advance our capabilities and drive progress in a distant future. However, climate change threatens to turn that inherent positivity of the human imagination into predictions of doom for our planetary future. How do we imagine the alterations that a future environment of unpredictable hurricanes, gradual sea-level rise, rapid temperature changes, heat and droughts, and loss of biodiversity may have on our social and cultural fabric? What role does technology play in these narratives of adaptation and evolution?

Recent work by Korean artists explore speculative narratives of proposed and projected futures using technological workflows made mainstream with works at Hyundai Artlab, MMCA, ACC Gwangju, and more. This media art revolution has brought Korea to the forefront of art-technology in Asia, with a view for empowering both established and emergent artists working in Korea. With Hong Kong in the midst of weather and environmental change, the work of Korean media artists working with speculative narratives about our reactions to a future of change gains greater relevance. The Korean Culture Center has long supported emerging Korean artists and aims to connect them with audiences worldwide. To engage the Hong Kong art and cultural community, Hong Kong Korean Cultural Center collaborated with the Studio For Narrative Spaces to curate the exhibition, bringing together five Korean artists and a Hong Kong collaborative art project that use speculative narratives and technological experimentation to reimagine possible post-climate futures through VR, sound, robotics, games, and multimedia installations.

"Koko Killing Island: Tour d’Epicure" is a VR stage where audience-performers become eco-tourists on a virtual island that has survived the climate crisis under the guidance of virtual hosts. "The Eggs" reimagines the egg as a symbol of spiritual transformation and collective resilience, weaving together Korean shamanic traditions, sociopolitical history, and cosmic imagery through paintings and sculptural installations. "Perceive (Panorama)" turns automobile routes, leaf patterns, stock-chart swings, and ocean tides into a continuous musical panorama that makes everyday speeds and orders newly perceptible. "Scope of the Cloud" is a game set at an aerial city "CLOUD" in 2051 built for climate refugees, where players need to choose whether to follow orders to eliminate birds or defy the rules. "Symbiotic Luminescence" imagines life rising from the deep sea to the surface, showing co-evolution and the fragile marine environment from a beyond human perspective. These works use VR, sound, game, and multimedia installation to present how Korean artists envision future worlds shaped by climate change. "Nonhumotion 2.0" presents two synchronized robots performing a ritualistic dance within a post-apocalyptic climate landscape, reimagining the Indigenous rain dance as a future ceremony where machines, rather than humans, plead for mercy from a collapsing planet.

We believe this exhibition will invite local audiences to experience Korean perspectives on climate change and future imaginaries by presenting these artworks. We also trust it will spark new conversations and mutual inspiration, encouraging Hong Kong artists exploring similar themes to exchange ideas, compare approaches, and form new collaborations with Korean artists.

人們在想像未來的科技發展時，常常持積極而充滿希望的態度。我們固然相信科技能推動文明進步，拓展人類能力，以致改變整個世界。然而，氣候變化正逐漸將這種原本充滿正向期待的願景，轉變為對地球命運的憂慮與警示。當不可預測的颶風、海平面上升、劇烈溫度變化、熱浪、乾旱，以及生物多樣性的消失成為日常，我們應如何想像未來環境對社會與文化結構帶來的改變？而科技，又在這些關於適應與演化的敘事之中扮演甚麼角色？

近年，多位韓國藝術家透過科技媒介與生成式工作流程，建構對未來的推想與投射，其作品曾於 Hyundai Artlab、韓國國立現代美術館（MMCA）、國立亞洲文化殿堂（ACC Gwangju）等機構展出。這場媒體藝術的革新，使韓國成為亞洲藝術與科技發展的重要前沿，同時亦持續支持新興與成熟藝術家的創作實踐。韓國文化中心一直致力支持韓國新銳藝術家，並推動其與全球觀眾建立交流。隨著香港正面對愈加顯著的極端天氣與環境變遷，韓國媒體藝術家以推想性敘事探討未來變化的作品，也因此與香港當下的處境產生更深層的共鳴。為促進香港藝術與文化社群之間的互動，香港韓國文化中心與Studio For Narrative Spaces合作策展，集結五位韓國藝術家與一組香港本地合作藝術計劃，透過 VR、聲音、機械裝置、遊戲與多媒體裝置藝術，以推想性敘事描繪後氣候時代的未來想像。

「Koko Killing Island: Tour d’Epicure」是一個虛擬實境舞台，觀眾將化身為生態觀光旅客，在虛擬導遊的帶領下探索一座於氣候危機後倖存的島嶼。「The Eggs」將「蛋」重新詮釋為靈性轉化與集體韌性的象徵，透過繪畫與雕塑裝置交織韓國宗教傳統、社會政治歷史與宇宙意象。「Perceive (Panorama)」把汽車路線、葉脈紋理、股市波動與海洋潮汐轉化為連續流動的聲音全景，使日常中的速度與秩序以嶄新方式被感知。「Scope of the Cloud」是一款設定於 2051 年空中城市「CLOUD」的遊戲，這座城市為氣候難民而建，而玩家需要在服從命令消滅鳥類，或違抗制度之間作出抉擇。「Symbiotic Luminescence」則想像深海生命浮現至海洋表層的未來景象，從超越人類中心的視角，呈現共生演化與脆弱海洋生態之間的關係。「Nonhumotion 2.0」則透過兩台機械人於末世氣候景觀中進行同步儀式性舞蹈，重新想像原住民祈雨儀式，描繪在生態崩壞的未來裡，向自然祈求憐憫的不再是人類，而是機械生命。這些作品透過 VR、聲音、遊戲與多媒體裝置等形式，呈現韓國藝術家如何想像一個受氣候變遷所塑造的未來世界。

我們相信，通過展出這些以氣候與科技為主題的作品，本次展覽將引領本地觀眾體驗韓國藝術家對氣候變遷與未來想像的視角。同時激發新的討論與靈感交流，鼓勵香港從事相關主題創作的藝術家與韓國藝術家彼此對話、比較不同方法，並建立新的合作關係。
