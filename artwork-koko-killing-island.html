Koko Killing Island: Tour d'Epicure

Inhwa Yeom, 2022. PC-based VR, installation, NFT.

Koko Killing Island: Tour d'Epicure is a VR stage where audience-performers become eco-tourists on a virtual island that has survived the climate crisis. Guided by virtual hosts, visitors enter a fictional tourism system that mixes ecological spectacle, social ritual, accessibility, and survival after environmental collapse.

The work places the audience inside an invented post-climate destination, asking how tourism, leisure, desire, and technological mediation might continue when climate crisis has already reshaped the conditions of everyday life.

2026 Korea-China-Japan Year of Cultural Exchanges

2025-2026 中日韓文化交流年

The “2025–2026 Korea-China-Japan Year of Cultural Exchanges” was officially proclaimed at the Trilateral Summit on May 27, 2024, under the shared recognition that culture serves as a bridge connecting the three nations. Jointly organized by the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism of the Republic of Korea, the Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology of Japan, and the Ministry of Culture and Tourism of the People’s Republic of China, the initiative is designed to deepen cultural exchange and strengthen people-to-people ties among the three countries.
